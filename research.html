Research Areas

Our interdisciplinary research spans the boundary between physics, chemistry, and biology — probing materials that defy simple classification.

01
Polymer Physics & Hydrogels

Investigating chain dynamics, network topology, and stimuli-responsive behavior in synthetic and biopolymer systems.

ViscoelasticitySwellingRheology
02
Colloidal Self-Assembly

Designing colloidal particles that spontaneously organize into functional structures with emergent optical and mechanical properties.

Patchy ParticlesCrystallization
03
Active & Living Matter

Understanding the collective motion of motile bacteria, cytoskeletal filaments, and synthetic active particles far from equilibrium.

MotilityNon-equilibrium
04
Liquid Crystals

Probing topological defects, phase transitions, and director field dynamics in thermotropic and lyotropic liquid crystalline systems.

DefectsBirefringence
05
Interfacial Phenomena

Exploring wetting, capillarity, and emulsion stability at soft interfaces — from Pickering emulsions to biological membranes.

WettingEmulsionsMembranes
06
Biophysics & Biomaterials

Bridging soft matter and biology: from extracellular matrix mechanics to the design of biomimetic scaffolds for regenerative medicine.
